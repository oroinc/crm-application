A. HISTORY OF THE SOFTWARE<br />
==========================<br />
<br />
Python was created in the early 1990s by Guido van Rossum at Stichting<br />
Mathematisch Centrum (CWI, see http://www.cwi.nl) in the Netherlands<br />
as a successor of a language called ABC.  Guido remains Python&#039;s<br />
principal author, although it includes many contributions from others.<br />
<br />
In 1995, Guido continued his work on Python at the Corporation for<br />
National Research Initiatives (CNRI, see http://www.cnri.reston.va.us)<br />
in Reston, Virginia where he released several versions of the<br />
software.<br />
<br />
In May 2000, Guido and the Python core development team moved to<br />
BeOpen.com to form the BeOpen PythonLabs team.  In October of the same<br />
year, the PythonLabs team moved to Digital Creations, which became<br />
Zope Corporation.  In 2001, the Python Software Foundation (PSF, see<br />
https://www.python.org/psf/) was formed, a non-profit organization<br />
created specifically to own Python-related Intellectual Property.<br />
Zope Corporation was a sponsoring member of the PSF.<br />
<br />
All Python releases are Open Source (see http://www.opensource.org for<br />
the Open Source Definition).  Historically, most, but not all, Python<br />
releases have also been GPL-compatible; the table below summarizes<br />
the various releases.<br />
